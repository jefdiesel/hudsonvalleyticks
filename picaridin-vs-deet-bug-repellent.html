Picaridin vs DEET: Which Bug Repellent Should You Choose?

Introduction

You're about to head out for a Hudson Valley hike. You know you need protection against ticks and mosquitoes. You reach for bug spray. But which one?

The bug repellent aisle is confusing. DEET, Picaridin, oil of lemon eucalyptus, "natural" sprays‚Äîwhich actually works? And which is safest for your family?

The good news: the CDC has clear recommendations. For tick prevention specifically, Picaridin has emerged as an excellent option‚Äîsometimes even better than DEET.

How Bug Repellents Work

Bug repellents don't kill insects like permethrin does. Instead, they make it harder for insects to find you. They mask your carbon dioxide signature or irritate the insect's sensory organs, causing it to fly or crawl away.

Different repellent chemicals work differently, which is why effectiveness varies.

DEET: The Gold Standard (Sort Of)

What DEET Does

  • Effective against mosquitoes, ticks, flies, chiggers, and other insects
  • Protection duration: 4-6 hours (depending on concentration)
  • Higher concentrations (30-40%) provide longer protection
  • Works in most climates

DEET Pros

  • Extensively tested over 50+ years
  • Very effective against many insects
  • Works at lower concentrations

DEET Cons

  • Can damage certain plastics and synthetic fabrics
  • Greasy feel on skin
  • Can cause skin irritation in sensitive people
  • Some people report a chemical odor

Picaridin: The Rising Star

What Picaridin Does

  • Repels mosquitoes, ticks, flies, and other insects
  • Similar effectiveness to DEET at comparable concentrations
  • Protection duration: 8+ hours (longer than DEET)
  • 20% Picaridin = 200 times more effective than oil of lemon eucalyptus

Picaridin Pros

  • Lighter, non-greasy feel
  • Doesn't damage plastics or synthetics
  • Longer protection duration than DEET
  • Less likely to cause skin irritation
  • No harsh chemical smell
  • Works great in humid climates

Picaridin Cons

  • Slightly less well-known than DEET
  • Can be harder to find in some areas
  • Slightly higher cost than some DEET products
üí° Pro Tip: For Hudson Valley residents, Picaridin is often the better choice because it lasts longer (especially important during all-day outdoor activities) and has a better feel on skin for frequent reapplication.

How to Apply Repellent Correctly

  1. Apply to exposed skin (arms, legs, neck)
  2. Apply to top of shoes and pant legs (ticks crawl up)
  3. Don't apply under clothing where ticks can hide
  4. Reapply after swimming or excessive sweating
  5. Use permethrin-treated clothing + Picaridin for best results
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake: Using bug spray alone without treating clothing. Bug spray doesn't kill ticks; it repels them. Ticks can still climb through untreated fabrics. Always combine repellent with permethrin-treated clothing.

For Children and Pregnant Women

Children 2 Months and Older

  • Picaridin 10% is safe for children 2+ months
  • DEET 10% is also safe for children 2+ months
  • Never use on hands (they put hands in mouth)
  • Apply to parent's hands, then rub on child

Infants Under 2 Months

  • Avoid repellents if possible
  • Use protective clothing instead
  • Keep them indoors during peak tick season if in high-risk areas

Natural Alternatives (Reality Check)

Oil of lemon eucalyptus (OLE), citronella, and other "natural" options are less effective. Oil of lemon eucalyptus has some effectiveness but requires more frequent reapplication.

üí° Pro Tip: "Natural" doesn't mean safer or better. The CDC recommends DEET and Picaridin because they're well-studied and effective‚Äînot because they're chemicals. Picaridin is actually derived from a natural compound found in pepper plants.

Conclusion

For Hudson Valley tick prevention, Picaridin 20% offers excellent protection, lasts longer than DEET, and feels better on skin. Combined with permethrin-treated clothing and regular tick checks, it's a solid part of your defense strategy.

Choose Picaridin for all-day outdoor activities. You'll appreciate the longer protection window and the light, non-greasy feel.
