Introduction

Why Treat Your Own Clothes?

  • Cost-effective: Treat existing clothes instead of buying new ones
  • Versatile: Apply to any fabric‚Äîjeans, hiking pants, boots, hats, even backpacks
  • Effective: Same active ingredient as factory-treated clothing
  • Flexible: Treat clothes seasonally as needed

How Permethrin Spray Works

Permethrin spray works exactly like factory treatment. When sprayed on fabrics, it dries and bonds to the fibers. It kills ticks on contact and lasts through multiple washings (typically 5-6 washings depending on the product).

Step-by-Step: How to Treat Clothes with Permethrin

What You'll Need

  • Permethrin spray (Sawyer or similar brand)
  • Outdoor space (garage, deck, yard)
  • Clothes or gear to treat
  • Hanger or laid flat
  • 30 minutes drying time

Step 1: Choose Your Location

Spray outside‚Äîgarage, deck, or yard. You want good ventilation. Do not spray indoors.

Step 2: Hang or Lay Out Clothes

Hang items on a hanger or lay flat on a table. Make sure all surfaces are accessible.

Step 3: Spray Evenly

Following the bottle instructions, spray until the fabric is slightly wet but not soaking. Cover:

  • Pants legs and waistband
  • Shirt sleeves and front/back
  • Socks and shoe uppers
  • Hat brims and band

Step 4: Let Dry Completely

Wait at least 4-6 hours (preferably overnight) before wearing. Full effectiveness requires drying time.

Step 5: Wait Before Washing

Don't wash treated clothes for at least 24 hours. This allows the permethrin to fully bond to the fibers.

üí° Pro Tip: Treat clothes the day before a hiking trip. That way they're dry and ready when you head out.

Safety: Is Permethrin Spray Safe?

For Humans

  • EPA-registered and safe when used as directed
  • Breaks down on human skin
  • Does not absorb through treated clothing
  • Safe to wear treated clothing against your skin once dry

For Pets

  • Safe for treated clothing around pets
  • Do not spray directly on animals
  • Let clothes dry completely before allowing pets near them
  • Some pets (especially cats) are more sensitive‚Äîuse caution

Storage and Handling

  • Keep out of reach of children and pets
  • Store in cool, dry place
  • Don't inhale fumes‚Äîspray outdoors
  • Wash hands after applying
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Never spray permethrin directly on your skin or as a body repellent. It's designed for fabric, not skin. Use skin repellents (Picaridin, DEET) on exposed skin instead.

Best Products to Treat

  • Hiking pants and shirts: Essential
  • Socks: Very important (ticks crawl up)
  • Boots: Important for ankle protection
  • Hats and caps: Good idea for head/neck protection
  • Backpack exterior: Helpful as additional barrier
  • Work gloves: Useful if doing yard work

Effectiveness: How Long Does It Last?

The permethrin treatment on clothes you've sprayed lasts:

  • 5-6 washings: At normal water temperature with regular detergent
  • Fewer if: Hot water, strong detergents, dry cleaning
  • Longer if: Cold water, gentle detergent, air drying

Most people treat clothes once per season, or when they stop working effectively.
