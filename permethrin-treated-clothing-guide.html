What is Permethrin and How Does It Work?

Permethrin is an EPA-registered synthetic insecticide derived from a natural compound found in chrysanthemum flowers. When factory-applied to clothing, it creates an invisible protective barrier that kills ticks, mosquitoes, and other insects on contact.

Unlike insect repellents (which you apply to your skin), permethrin actually kills the insect rather than repelling it. Ticks that land on your clothing and come into contact with permethrin are killed within minutes‚Äîbefore they can crawl to your skin or bite you.

The Science Behind It

  • Quick action: Most ticks die within 30 seconds to 2 minutes of contact
  • Multiple insects: Effective against ticks, mosquitoes, chiggers, and mites
  • Non-toxic to humans: Breaks down on human skin and doesn't penetrate treated clothing
  • Long-lasting: Factory treatment lasts through 70+ washings
üí° Pro Tip: Permethrin is so safe that it's used in products to treat head lice on children. It's one of the few substances recommended by both the CDC and pediatricians for tick prevention.

Why Permethrin-Treated Clothing is Your Best Defense

The Hudson Valley experiences peak tick activity from spring through fall, with the highest concentrations in May-June and September-October. During these months, ticks are actively seeking hosts in our forests, trails, and even backyards.

Effectiveness Statistics

  • Permethrin-treated clothing: 99.3% effective at preventing tick attachment
  • Picaridin skin repellent alone: 95% effective
  • Layered approach (treated clothing + skin repellent + tick checks): 99%+ effective

How Long Does Permethrin Treatment Last?

One of the biggest advantages of permethrin-treated clothing is its durability. Factory-applied permethrin lasts through approximately 70 standard washings at normal water temperature.

What Affects Durability?

  • Water temperature: Hot water reduces effectiveness faster than cold or warm
  • Detergent type: Strong detergents break down permethrin faster
  • Dry cleaning: Professional dry cleaning removes permethrin completely
  • Storage: Store in cool, dry place away from direct sunlight

Best practice: Wash in cold water with regular detergent and air dry when possible. This extends the life of your permethrin treatment to the full 70+ washings.

Permethrin-treated clothing works best as part of a layered defense strategy. The CDC recommends:

  3. Layer 3: Thorough tick checks after outdoor activities
